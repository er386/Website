F80 M3 Overview

Introduction: The F80 M3 produced from 2014-2018, marked the return of the M3 sedan as the coupe became its own model, the M4. The F80 introduced turbocharging to the M3 line, signaling a new era of high-efficiency performance.

Engine & Performance: Under the hood is the S55 3.0L twin-turbo inline-six, producing 425 hp and 406 lb-ft of torque. With the optional Competition Package, power increased to 444 hp. Paired with either a 6-speed manual or 7-speed DCT, the F80 was capable of 0-60 in as little as 3.8 seconds.

Track Times & Handling: The F80 set Nürburgring lap times under 7:50 in Competition spec, while the lighter, 2 door M4 CS brought lap times down to 7:52. While some missed the high revving feel of past models, the S55 delivered massive torque and quicker lap times. Adaptive M suspension and improved electronics gave the F80 sharp precision, though some enthusiasts felt it was less raw than earlier M3s.

Notable Awards & Recognition: The F80 M3 earned multiple awards, including “Car of the Year” nominations and “Best Sports Sedan” accolades from Car and Driver. It helped redefine the segment by balancing track-ready performance with everyday usability and tech advancements.

Common Issues:

  • Crank Hub Spinning: A known issue in tuned or aggressively driven cars. The fix involves a pinned or upgraded crank hub which is not cheap, but critical for reliability.
  • Turbo Rattle: Wastegate rattle can be annoying, though often harmless. Still, it's worth checking on test drives.
  • Rod Bearings: Less common than previous gens but still a concern for high-mileage or hard-driven examples.
  • Carbon Buildup: Like many direct injection engines, carbon can build up on intake valves over time and require walnut blasting.

The F80 is a highly tunable platform and one of the most powerful and capable M3s ever made. It's perfect for those wanting modern tech and speed without sacrificing the spirit of the M division.
