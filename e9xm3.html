E9X M3 Overview

Introduction: The E9X M3, produced from 2008 to 2013, marked a huge shift for the M3. For the first and only time, BMW equipped the M3 with a naturally aspirated V8 engine. The E9X came in three body styles: E90 (sedan), E92 (coupe), and E93 (convertible).

Engine & Performance: The S65 4.0L V8 engine delivered 414 hp and 295 lb-ft of torque, revving to a thrilling 8,400 RPM. It was paired with a 6-speed manual or 7-speed dual-clutch (DCT) transmission. The engine was derived from BMW's F1 program and is widely regarded as one of the most exciting M engines ever.

Track Times & Handling: The 0-60 mph on the E9X came in at about 4.1 seconds with the DCT. Nürburgring lap times ranged around 8:05. The E9X chassis offered excellent feedback and was more refined than its predecessors with the cost of being heavier. The optional Competition Package on the facelift version tightened handling even further.

Notable Awards & Recognition: The E9X M3 earned praise from nearly every automotive publication for its balance of luxury and raw performance. It won Motor Trend's Best Driver's Car and was named one of the greatest driver's cars of the 2000s by Top Gear.

Common Issues:

  • Rod Bearings: Just like the E46, the E9X's S65 V8 is known for rod bearing wear, which is a costly but preventable issue with regular maintenance or aftermarket replacements.
  • Throttle Actuators: These electronic parts can fail and are expensive to replace, affecting engine performance and drivability.
  • High Running Costs: The V8 is thrilling but thirsty. Expect higher fuel and maintenance costs compared to inline six generations.
  • DCT Fluid Changes: Many early owners didn't follow proper fluid service intervals, leading to long-term DCT issues.

Despite these concerns, the E9X M3 is often considered the last of the “pure” M3s before turbocharging took over, making it a future classic.
