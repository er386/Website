G80 M3 Overview

Introduction: The G80 M3 debuted in 2021 and brought one of the most dramatic visual changes in M3 history with its massive kidney grille that stirred controversy. Under the surface, however, the G80 is one of the most advanced and capable M cars ever built.

Engine & Performance: The G80 uses the S58 engine: a 3.0L twin-turbo inline-six. In base trim it produces 473 hp and 406 lb-ft of torque with an option of a 6-speed manual transmission. The Competition version bumps output to 503 hp and 479 lb-ft, paired with an 8-speed automatic. Optional xDrive all-wheel-drive pushes the 0-60 time as low as 3.4 seconds.

The chassis is extremely rigid, and the electronic dampers deliver precision without sacrificing comfort. Despite its size, the G80 handles like a much smaller car.

Notable Awards & Recognition: Critics have praised the G80's dual personality: a plush, tech-filled daily driver and a focused, beastly performer. Car and Driver included it in their 10Best list multiple years, and it continues to rank at the top of sports sedan comparisons worldwide.

Common Issues:

  • Weight: At over 3,800 lbs, the G80 is heavier than older M3s. While performance isn't compromised, some purists miss the light feel of earlier models.
  • Brake Squeal: The optional carbon ceramic brakes can squeak during light use, especially in city driving.
  • Tech Overload: Some owners report frustration with too many driver aids and settings, though they can be turned off or customized.
  • Grille Controversy: While not a mechanical issue, the styling continues to polarize the BMW community.

Overall, the G80 M3 is a powerhouse of modern engineering, delivering staggering performance, daily drivability, and customizable dynamics, and it still offers a manual gearbox for purists.
